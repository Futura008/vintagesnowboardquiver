Vintage Snowboard Quiver

Don't just hang 'em, shred 'em

Welcome to Vintage Snowboards Quiver! This site is made for all snowboard enthusiasts who love surf inspirated snowboard shapes and retro style with neon colours.

Get your vintage snowboard out of the garage, tighten up your Sorel's and have fun!

I have collected vintage snowboards for a few years now, but I don't just collect them - I ride them. There are a few highligts of my vintage snowboard quiver. Enjoy!

Burton Elite 1988

Burton Elite is a piece of snowboarding history with a swallowtail and steel edges. The board has a concave base so it's very hard to ride.

Sims Switchblade 1988

Sims boards have definitely place in my quiver. I remember when I first saw a variety of these on the local hillside. It's a very heavy and stiff board. Neon is never out of style.

Sims 1510 Blade ATV 1989

Nice board with cool graphics! The development of snowboarding had made a huge steps in the late 80's.

Burton Cruzer 1988

Burton had advertised Cruzer to "rider who wants a board that is forgiving enough to learn on, but is capable of high performance riding on diffucult terrain".

Sims 1610 Half Pipe Freestyle 1989

This is a latest addition on my snowboard quiver. The shape was groundbreaking at the time.

Burton Air 6.1 1992

One of my favourite snowboards with beautiful colors. An absolute joy to ride!

Burton Kelly Air & Micro 1992

1992 was the last year for Burton to use a 5 hole pattern for bindings. I ride Kelly Air with vintage snowboard plates which allow me to use contemporary 4x4 bindings. Craig Kelly inspired a lot of people during his lifetime. Kelly Air was his 4th pro model.
